Passage 9

THE IMPORTANCE OF
ORAL COMMUNICATION






The ability to communicate is a basic skill that one must master in order to succeed in life. We face many opportunities to communicate orally each day. It begins formally in elementary school where children are encouraged to participate in school plays. One may think that these opportunities are not important, that it’s just child’s play. But in reality, we learn to communicate through play. In school plays, children learn how to perform in front of people.

In high school, teachers encourage children to write essays and read books, so we can expand our knowledge and form opinions. Developing good oral communication is important in the workplace. The ability to express ideas, and thoughts in a clear and precise manner is a positive attribute that is common among business leaders.

Being able to give orders, and manage human resources is made easier when one communicates well orally. Of course, oral communication is only part of effective communication.

The use of gestures, body language, and facial expressions are also part of effective communication, but oral communication is the part that solicits actions and reactions. So, remember next time you have to make a presentation in front of people, choose your words carefully, and relax, imagine you’re back in school performing in a school play.
