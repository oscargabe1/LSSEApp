Expressions

1. IN A JAM

En dificultades, en problemas.

Don’t steal anything or you’ll be in a jam with the police.

2. TO PLAY HOOKY

Irse de pinta.

When I was in high school I played hooky .

3. (TO) GET LOST

Perderse; ¡Márchate!.

I got lost in Mexico City; it’s a very big city.

4. TELL IT TO THE MARINES

A otro con ese cuento.

Come on Peter, tell it to the marines , I don’t believe your story.

5. TO PUT ON AIRS

Creerse mucho; darse demasiada importancia.

Mario is not very smart; he is just putting on airs .

6. TO TAKE FRENCH LEAVE

Irse sin despedirse.

In our last party I didn't realize when Lucy left the party, she just took a french leave.

7. (TO BE) TO MAKE A POOR SHOWING

(Haber) Poca gente; hacer un mal papel.

There was a poor showing at the convention.

8. TO BE EVEN

Estar a mano; ser iguales; estar parejos.

Now we are even with Mike.

9. TO TALK UP

Recomendar, elogiar.

The salesman was trying to talk up the price of the car.

10. TO BE WORDY

Ser elocuente, redundante; ser rollero.

His story about his life was wordy .
