Passage 1

SAMUEL CLEMENS:
MARK TWAIN







His real name was Samuel Clemens, but everybody knew him as Mark Twain. Mark Twain was a famous American writer. Mark Twain was so famous in his days that there are many stories about him that have survived. Some of these stories may be true; others may be false. They are all very interesting. The following are two such stories:

Once Mark Twain wanted to borrow a certain book from a friend. The friend told him that he never permitted anyone to borrow any of his books from his personal library. If anyone wanted to use a book from his personal library, he must not remove it from it.

A few days later, this same friend wanted to borrow Mark Twain’s lawn mower in order to mow his lawn. Mark Twain then told him that he never permitted anyone to take his lawn mower from his yard. If the friend wanted to use the lawn mower, he must use it on Mark Twain’s own grass.

Here is yet another story of the famous Mark Twain:

One morning, when Mark Twain was dressing, he found that his shoes were covered with mud. He called his servant and said:

“My shoes are covered with mud. Why didn’t you clean them last night?” replied Mark Twain. “I thought it was useless to clean them in such bad weather, they will only get dirty again in a short time” the servant replied.

Later that day, when the servant came for his dinner, there was no food on the table for him. He went to Mark Twain and asked him why there was no food for him. “I thought it was useless for you to eat,” replied Mark Twain “because you will only get hungry again in a short time.”
