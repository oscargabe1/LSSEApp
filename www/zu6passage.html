BIG BEN






Big Ben is the name of the famous clock tower that chimes the hour in London, England. It is located in the British House of Parliament. It was named for Sir Benjamin Hall, the chief Commissioner of Public Works. He was the minister responsible for installing the clock in its tower. The British Broadcasting regularly broadcasts all over the world the famous deep, resonant boom of the bell.

The way to Big Ben is up a spiral staircase of exactly 374 steps. The Roman numerals on the clock face are two feet high and the pendulum is 13 feet long. Big Ben itself weighs 13 tons. When the clock was installed, two men used to wind it every week. Now it runs on electricity.
