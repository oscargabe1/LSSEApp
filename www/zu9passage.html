MONA LISA






The world’s most favorite portrait is the “Mona Lisa.” It was painted by Leonardo da Vinci (1452- 1519) between 1503 and 1506. It hangs in the Louvre museum, which formally served as the palace of the French King. The Louvre Museum displays priceless art treasures and antiques.

The Mona Lisa is also called La Gioconda because the sitter’s married name was Giocondo. Except for two occasions, it has been in France ever since 1516 when Leonardo da Vinci left Italy to settle in France. Once, in 1911, the picture was stolen from the Louvre and found two years later in Italy. The second time it left France for 26 days, in 1960 on a fantastic, well guarded, and highly insured, visit to the United States as a guest of President John F. Kennedy.

Leonardo da Vinci began painting the Mona Lisa when the young girl was 24 and he was 51. She used to come to the great master’s studio in the evening when the light was soft. Over the three years, Leonardo became fascinated by his model. Perhaps that was the reason why her husband, Francesco del Giocondo, never received the finished portrait. The artist always made the excuse that he had not quite finished it. He carried it with him whenever he went to Milan, to Rome and finally, to France. Where King Francis I offered the artist a palace in the beautiful Loire Valley, the Chateau de Cloux.

It is believed that Francis I paid 4,000 gold crowns for the Mona Lisa. However, it was not until after Leonardo’s death that the king was able, at last, to possess the painting of the Florentine lady with the enigmatic smile. Thereafter, it remained in the possession of the kings and emperors of France. The picture hung at Fontainebleau, at Versailles and at the Tuileries. Then in 1800 it hung on the wall of Napoleon Bonaparte’s bedroom.
