Questions & Answers

TELEPHONE CALLS




1. Good morning. Is this Mr. Adams' office?

Yes, this is Mr. Adams' office.
2. May I help you?

Yes, you may.
3. Is Mr. Adams' in?

No, he is not in at the moment. But may I take your message.
4. Who is calling?

I am Mr. Brady's secretary.
5. Is your call a matter of urgency?

Yes, I must speak with Mr. Adams at once!
6. Will you hold, please?

Of course, thank you.
7. With whom do you wish to speak?

I would like to speak to Mrs. Johnson.
8. Would you like to leave a message?

No, thank you. I will call back later.
9. Would you ask Mrs. Johnson to return my call?

Yes, I will gladly give her your message.
10. Would you please tell Mrs. Johnson that Mr. Williams called?

I sure will, Mrs. Williams. Have a nice day.
11. When will she be back?

Mrs. Johnson will be back in about an hour.
12. May I use your phone to make a local telephone call?

Sure, no problem.
13. Operator, I wish to place a collect call to Mexico City.

Sure, what is the number?.
14. What number are you trying to reach?

The number is (123) 444-5678.
15. Who is calling?

This is Alice Smith.
16. Where are you calling from?

I am calling from Florida.
17. The line is busy. Do you wish to try again?

Yes, please.
18. May I speak to Mr. Lewis?

I'm sorry; he is not in at the moment.
19. May I ask who is calling?

This is Alice Smith from Florida.
20. Hello, Baker & Spaulding. How can I help you?

This is Peter Jackson. Can I have extension 3421?
21. Certainly, I'll put you through.

Thanks.
22. Frank Jacob's office. Helen speaking?

This is Peter Jackson calling, is Frank in?
23. I'm afraid Mr. Jacobs is out at the moment. Can I take a message?

Yes, could you please ask him to call me at 555-8329.
24. Could you repeat the number please?

Yes, that's 555-8329, and this is Peter Jackson.
25. Could you make sure that he gets this message? It's quite urgent.

Certainly, I'll make sure Mr. Jacobs get this as soon as possible.
